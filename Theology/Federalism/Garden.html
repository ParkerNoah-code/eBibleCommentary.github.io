The Garden of Eden

The Garden

Genesis 2: "7 The LORD God formed man from the dust of the ground, and breathed into his nostrils the breath of life; and man became a living soul. 8 The LORD God planted a garden eastward, in Eden, and there he put the man whom he had formed. 9 Out of the ground the LORD God made every tree to grow that is pleasant to the sight, and good for food, including the tree of life in the middle of the garden and the tree of the knowledge of good and evil.

10 A river went out of Eden to water the garden; and from there it was parted, and became the source of four rivers. 11 The name of the first is Pishon: it flows through the whole land of Havilah, where there is gold; 12 and the gold of that land is good. Bdellium and onyx stone are also there. 13 The name of the second river is Gihon. It is the same river that flows through the whole land of Cush. 14 The name of the third river is Hiddekel. This is the one which flows in front of Assyria. The fourth river is the Euphrates.

15 The LORD God took the man, and put him into the garden of Eden to cultivate and keep it. 16 The LORD God commanded the man, saying, “You may freely eat of every tree of the garden; 17 but you shall not eat of the tree of the knowledge of good and evil; for in the day that you eat of it, you will surely die.” "

Garden as Temple

Ezekiel 28: "11 Moreover the LORD’s word came to me, saying, 12 “Son of man, take up a lamentation over the king of Tyre, and tell him, ‘The Lord GOD says:

“You were the seal of full measure, full of wisdom, and perfect in beauty. 13 You were in Eden, the garden of God. Every precious stone adorned you: ruby, topaz, emerald, chrysolite, onyx, jasper, sapphire, turquoise, and beryl. Gold work of tambourines and of pipes was in you. They were prepared in the day that you were created. 14 You were the anointed cherub who covers. Then I set you up on the holy mountain of God. You have walked up and down in the middle of the stones of fire. 15 You were perfect in your ways from the day that you were created, until unrighteousness was found in you.’” "

Indicators that Eden was a temple:

  1. Mountaintop location
  2. The tabernacle and temple use garden imagery
  3. Precious stones are also in sanctuaries and priestly garments
  4. Priestly language is used of guarding and keeping
  5. God's presence dwelt there

G. K. Beale, Adam as the First Priest in Eden as the Garden Temple, SBJT 22.2 (2018), 9-24.

Mountaintops are where God and man meet:

Mt. Sinai

God reveals His glory and holiness; Moses meets God face-to-face. God's covenant with Israel, the giving of the Law (Ex. 19:23; Deut. 33:2).

Mt. Moriah

Abraham's test of faith and the provision of a ram as a substitute for Isaac was in the land of Moriah (Gen. 22:2–14). Later on, Mt. Moriah was the site where Solomon's Temple was built (2 Chr. 3:1).

Mt. Zion

Represents God's dwelling with His people in Jerusalem.

Temple worship and the central place of God's presence: (Pss. 43:3; 48:1; 87:1; 99:9; Ezek. 20:40).

Eschatological significance as the place of ultimate salvation (Isa. 27:13; 56:7; 57:13; 66:20).

Mt. Carmel

Elijah's confrontation with the prophets of Baal (1 Kgs. 18:16–45).

The Sermon on the Mount

Jesus' proclamation of the Kingdom of God and moral teachings (Matt. 5–7). A pivotal moment where Christ, as the greater Moses, interprets the Law with divine authority.

The Mount of Transfiguration

Jesus is revealed in His divine glory to Peter, James, and John alongside Moses and Elijah (Matt. 17:1–8; Mark 9:2–8; Luke 9:28–36).

The Mount of Olives

Prophetic discourse about judgment and the end of the age (Matt. 24–25; Mark 13; Luke 21).

Jesus' prayer and agony before His arrest (Matt. 26:30–46; Mark 14:26–42; Luke 22:39–46).

Ascension of Christ into heaven (Acts 1:9–12).

High Places

Often associated with idolatrous worship, which God forbade (Deut. 12:2; Num. 33:52; 1 Kgs. 3:2; 12:31; 14:23; Ps. 78:58; Jer. 7:31).

The New Jerusalem

A vision of God's ultimate dwelling with humanity, descending from heaven on a high mountain (Rev. 21:10).

Other Relevant Examples

Mt. Nebo: Moses views the Promised Land before his death, signifying God's promises and judgment (Deut. 34:1–4).

Mt. Gerizim and Mt. Ebal: Sites of covenant renewal and blessings/curses during Joshua's time (Deut. 27–28; Josh. 8:30–35).
