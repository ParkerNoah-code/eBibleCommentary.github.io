Confessions

1. 1537 Treatise on the Power and Primacy of the Pope Treatise - Philip Melanchthon wrote this treatise as a supplement to the Augsburg Confession.

2. 1647 Westminster Confession - Formulated by the Westminster Assembly, this is a cornerstone document for many Presbyterian churches.

3. 1658 Savoy Declaration - An adaptation of the Westminster Confession by English Congregationalists, modifying the sections on church government to reflect Congregationalist polity.

4. 1689 London Baptist Confession - Also known as the Second London Baptist Confession, it closely mirrors the Westminster Confession but differs on baptism and church government.

5. 1833 New Hampshire Confession - This confession was influential among Baptists in the northeastern United States and reflects a moderate Calvinism.

6. 1858 Abstract of Principles - This confession was adopted by the Southern Baptist Theological Seminary and reflects a strong commitment to Reformed theology, including the doctrines of grace and the authority of Scripture.

7. 1934 Barmen Declaration - Crafted by Karl Barth and other German theologians, this document was a response to the increasing influence of National Socialism over the German Protestant churches affirming the church's allegiance to Christ over any earthly authority.

8. 1978 Chicago Statement on Biblical Inerrancy - The Chicago Statement sets forth a series of articles that define inerrancy, explain its implications, and respond to common objections.

9. 1996 Cambridge Declaration - Though not a confession in the traditional sense, this statement by the Alliance of Confessing Evangelicals calls for a return to the five solas of the Reformation.
