Confessions

1. 1647 Westminster Confession - Formulated by the Westminster Assembly, this is a cornerstone document for many Presbyterian churches.

2. 1689 London Baptist Confession - Also known as the Second London Baptist Confession, it closely mirrors the Westminster Confession but differs on baptism and church government.

3. 1858 Abstract of Principles - This confession was adopted by the Southern Baptist Theological Seminary and reflects a strong commitment to Reformed theology, including the doctrines of grace and the authority of Scripture.

4. 1934 Barmen Declaration - Crafted by Karl Barth and other German theologians, this document was a response to the increasing influence of National Socialism over the German Protestant churches affirming the church's allegiance to Christ over any earthly authority.

5. 1978 Chicago Statement on Biblical Inerrancy - The Chicago Statement sets forth a series of articles that define inerrancy, explain its implications, and respond to common objections.

6. 1996 Cambridge Declaration - Though not a confession in the traditional sense, this statement by the Alliance of Confessing Evangelicals calls for a return to the five solas of the Reformation.
