Systematic Theology:

Logic: The study of correct reasoning. What are the rules of logic? How can we derive valid conclusions? How do we distinguish between good and bad arguments?

Epistemology: The study of knowledge. What is truth? Can our beliefs be justified? How do we know what we know? Can we trust our senses and reasoning?

Metaphyics: The study of the nature of ultimate reality. What is the nature of being, existence, causality, space, time, substance? Are there abstract entities?

Mind: The study of the nature of the mind. What is consciousness? What is the relationship between mind and matter? Do we free will?

Value: The study of ethics and values. What is the nature of right and wrong? Is morality objective? What is the nature of art and beauty?

Science: The study of the nature and methodology of science. What is the scientific method? What is the nature of scientific explanations? How does science relate to other forms of knowing?

Religion: The study of questions related to God. Does God exist? What is the nature of God? Can we have knowledge about God? What is the relationship between faith and reason? How can the problem of evil be answered?

Language: The study of language. What is the nature of language, meaning, and communication? How does language relate to thought?

History: The study of history and historical knowledge. How do we investigate and interpret the past? Does history have a purpose? What is the nature of narrative and our historical consciousness as it relates to history? In what way can history and the descriptions of historical events be described as true?

Political: The study of the organization and governance of society. What are the rights and responsibilities of citizens living in a society? Where does a government derive it's authority from? What does the ideal state look like? What is the proper relationship between church and state?
