What is theology?

The word "theology" is commonly used to describe a more theoretical displine looking at the existence and sovereignty of God, creation, and other similar topics. Christian theology considers matters from the vantage point of Scripture and is concerned with a broader range of topics like ethics, Bible translation, and redemption:

Theology is the science of God and of the relations between God and the creation. Theology is the science of the sciences because it reveals the underlying ground which all other sciences require for their complete explanation.

Christian theology is the application of Scripture by believers to all aspects of life.1 This includes the normative commands of Scripture, the existential experiences of man, and the situational application of Scritpure to varying aspects of life. It's goal is to allow us to know and serve God, meet the spiritual needs of his people and the world, and to promote godliness.
