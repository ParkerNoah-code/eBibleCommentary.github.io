I. The Superiority of Christ (1:1-3:6)

E. Perfected Salvation through Him (2:10-18)

Ver 10. For in that he subjected all things to him, he left nothing that is not subject to him. But now we don’t yet see all things subjected to him.

Ver 11. For both he who sanctifies and those who are sanctified are all from one, for which cause he is not ashamed to call them brothers,

Ver 12. saying, “I will declare your name to my brothers. Among the congregation I will sing your praise.”

Ver 13. Again, “I will put my trust in him.” Again, “Behold, here I am with the children whom God has given me.”

Ver 14. Since then the children have shared in flesh and blood, he also himself in the same way partook of the same, that through death he might bring to nothing him who had the power of death, that is, the devil,

Ver 15. and might deliver all of them who through fear of death were all their lifetime subject to bondage.

Ver 16. For most certainly, he doesn’t give help to angels, but he gives help to the offspring of Abraham.

Ver 17. Therefore he was obligated in all things to be made like his brothers, that he might become a merciful and faithful high priest in things pertaining to God, to make atonement for the sins of the people.

Ver 18. For in that he himself has suffered being tempted, he is able to help those who are tempted.
