II. Maturity and Holding Fast (3:7-6:20)

J. Sure and Steadfast Anchor (6:13-20)

Ver 13. For when God made a promise to Abraham, since he could swear by no one greater, he swore by himself,

Ver 14. saying, “Surely blessing I will bless you, and multiplying I will multiply you.”

Ver 15. Thus, having patiently endured, he obtained the promise.

Ver 16. For men indeed swear by a greater one, and in every dispute of theirs the oath is final for confirmation.

Ver 17. In this way God, being determined to show more abundantly to the heirs of the promise the immutability of his counsel, interposed with an oath,

Ver 18. that by two immutable things, in which it is impossible for God to lie, we may have a strong encouragement, who have fled for refuge to take hold of the hope set before us.

Ver 19. This hope we have as an anchor of the soul, a hope both sure and steadfast and entering into that which is within the veil,

Ver 20. where as a forerunner Jesus entered for us, having become a high priest forever after the order of Melchizedek.
