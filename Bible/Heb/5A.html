V. Concluding Exhortations (12:4-13:25)

A. Disciplined and Holy (12:4-17)

Ver 4. You have not yet resisted to blood, striving against sin.

Ver 5. You have forgotten the exhortation which reasons with you as with children, “My son, don’t take lightly the chastening of the Lord, nor faint when you are reproved by him;

Ver 6. for whom the Lord loves, he disciplines, and chastises every son whom he receives.”

Ver 7. It is for discipline that you endure. God deals with you as with children, for what son is there whom his father doesn’t discipline?

Ver 8. But if you are without discipline, of which all have been made partakers, then you are illegitimate, and not children.

Ver 9. Furthermore, we had the fathers of our flesh to chasten us, and we paid them respect. Shall we not much rather be in subjection to the Father of spirits and live?

Ver 10. For they indeed for a few days disciplined us as seemed good to them, but he for our profit, that we may be partakers of his holiness.

Ver 11. All chastening seems for the present to be not joyous but grievous; yet afterward it yields the peaceful fruit of righteousness to those who have been trained by it.

Ver 12-13. Therefore lift up the hands that hang down and the feeble knees, and make straight paths for your feet, so what is lame may not be dislocated, but rather be healed.

Ver 14. Follow after peace with all men, and the sanctification without which no man will see the Lord,

Ver 15. looking carefully lest there be any man who falls short of the grace of God, lest any root of bitterness springing up trouble you and many be defiled by it,

Ver 16. lest there be any sexually immoral person or profane person, like Esau, who sold his birthright for one meal.

Ver 17. For you know that even when he afterward desired to inherit the blessing, he was rejected, for he found no place for a change of mind though he sought it diligently with tears.
