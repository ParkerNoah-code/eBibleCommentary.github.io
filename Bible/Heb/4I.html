IV. Faith: Examples and Exhortation (10:19-12:3)

I. Faith: Exodus to Promised Land (11:23-31)

Ver 23. By faith Moses, when he was born, was hidden for three months by his parents, because they saw that he was a beautiful child; and they were not afraid of the king’s commandment.

Ver 24. By faith Moses, when he had grown up, refused to be called the son of Pharaoh’s daughter,

Ver 25. choosing rather to share ill treatment with God’s people than to enjoy the pleasures of sin for a time,

Ver 26. considering the reproach of Christ greater riches than the treasures of Egypt; for he looked to the reward.

Ver 27. By faith he left Egypt, not fearing the wrath of the king; for he endured, as seeing him who is invisible.

Ver 28. By faith he kept the Passover and the sprinkling of the blood, that the destroyer of the firstborn should not touch them.

Ver 29. By faith they passed through the Red Sea as on dry land. When the Egyptians tried to do so, they were swallowed up.

Ver 30. By faith the walls of Jericho fell down after they had been encircled for seven days.

Ver 31. By faith Rahab the prostitute didn’t perish with those who were disobedient, having received the spies in peace.
