II. Maturity and Holding Fast (3:7-6:20)

G. Moving on to Maturity (5:11-6:3)

Ver 11. About him we have many words to say, and hard to interpret, seeing you have become dull of hearing.

Ver 12. For although by this time you should be teachers, you again need to have someone teach you the rudiments of the first principles of the revelations of God. You have come to need milk, and not solid food.

Ver 13. For everyone who lives on milk is not experienced in the word of righteousness, for he is a baby.

Ver 14. But solid food is for those who are full grown, who by reason of use have their senses exercised to discern good and evil.

Ver 1a. Therefore leaving the teaching of the first principles of Christ, let’s press on to perfection—

Ver 1b-2. not laying again a foundation of repentance from dead works, of faith toward God, of the teaching of baptisms, of laying on of hands, of resurrection of the dead, and of eternal judgment.

Ver 3. This will we do, if God permits.
