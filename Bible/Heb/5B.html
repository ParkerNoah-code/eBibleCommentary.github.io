V. Concluding Exhortations (12:4-13:25)

B. Mount Zion: Heavenly City (12:18-24)

Ver 18. lest there be any sexually immoral person or profane person, like Esau, who sold his birthright for one meal.

Ver 19. lest there be any sexually immoral person or profane person, like Esau, who sold his birthright for one meal.

Ver 20. for they could not stand that which was commanded, “If even an animal touches the mountain, it shall be stoned”.

Ver 21. So fearful was the appearance that Moses said, “I am terrified and trembling.”

Ver 22. But you have come to Mount Zion and to the city of the living God, the heavenly Jerusalem, and to innumerable multitudes of angels,

Ver 23. to the festal gathering and assembly of the firstborn who are enrolled in heaven, to God the Judge of all, to the spirits of just men made perfect,

Ver 24. to Jesus, the mediator of a new covenant, and to the blood of sprinkling that speaks better than that of Abel.
