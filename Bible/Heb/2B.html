II. Maturity and Holding Fast (3:7-6:20)

B. Take Heed lest you Fall (3:12-19)

Ver 12. Beware, brothers, lest perhaps there might be in any one of you an evil heart of unbelief, in falling away from the living God;

Ver 13. but exhort one another day by day, so long as it is called “today”, lest any one of you be hardened by the deceitfulness of sin.

Ver 14. For we have become partakers of Christ, if we hold the beginning of our confidence firm to the end,

Ver 15. while it is said, “Today if you will hear his voice, don’t harden your hearts, as in the rebellion.”

Ver 16. For who, when they heard, rebelled? Wasn’t it all those who came out of Egypt led by Moses?

Ver 17. With whom was he displeased forty years? Wasn’t it with those who sinned, whose bodies fell in the wilderness?

Ver 18. To whom did he swear that they wouldn’t enter into his rest, but to those who were disobedient?

Ver 19. We see that they weren’t able to enter in because of unbelief.
