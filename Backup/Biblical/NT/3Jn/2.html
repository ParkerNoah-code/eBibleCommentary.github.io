3 John

II. Faithfulness of Gaius Commended (3-4)

3. For I rejoiced greatly when brothers came and testified about your truth, even as you walk in truth.

The truth is familiarly used to signify the pure doctrine of Christianity, which in its principal design aims at correspondent practice. That his children, i.e. such as had been converted to Christ by his ministry, of whom it appears Gaius was one, did walk in the truth (an apt expression of such correspondent practice).

4. I have no greater joy than this: to hear about my children walking in truth.

This was a great matter of joy to this holy apostle, especially when the godly lives of such, to whose conversion he had been instrumental, were so observable, as to gain them a testimony from all others that knew them, as it was in the present instance (see 1 Cor 4:15).
