Psalm 1

This psalm, though without a title, may reasonably be thought to be a psalm of David; since the next psalm, which is also without a title, is ascribed to him (Acts 4:25); and since both are joined together as one psalm by the Jews (Acts 4:25); and since this is the general preface to the whole book, which is chiefly of David's penning.

Ver 1. Blessed is the man who doesn’t walk in the counsel of the wicked,
nor stand on the path of sinners,
nor sit in the seat of scoffers;

Blessed is the man. The words may be rendered, "O, the blessednesses of the man", or "of this man"; he is doubly blessed, a thrice happy and blessed man; blessed in things temporal and spiritual; happy in this world, and in that to come.

He is to be praised and commended as a good man, so the Targum: "The goodness, or, Oh, the goodness of the man;" or as others, "Oh, the right goings or happy progress, or prosperous success of the man," who answers to the following descriptions; which right walking of his is next observed, and his prosperity in Psalms 1:3. Some have interpreted this psalm of Christ, and indeed these are properly spoken of him.

Ver 2. but his delight is in Yahweh’s law.
On his law he meditates day and night.

Ver 3. He will be like a tree planted by the streams of water,
that produces its fruit in its season,
whose leaf also does not wither.
Whatever he does shall prosper.

Ver 4. The wicked are not so,
but are like the chaff which the wind drives away.

Ver 5. Therefore the wicked shall not stand in the judgment,
nor sinners in the congregation of the righteous.

Ver 6. For Yahweh knows the way of the righteous,
but the way of the wicked shall perish.
