Apologetics

The Inescapability of Presuppositions: Every worldview begins with foundational beliefs that are assumed rather than proven. For presuppositional apologetics, the Christian worldview is the necessary starting point.

The Authority of Scripture: The Bible is the ultimate standard of truth. All reasoning and argumentation must be grounded in the truth of Scripture, which is self-authenticating and provides the basis for all knowledge and understanding.

The Impossibility of the Contrary: Without the presupposition of the Christian God, one cannot make sense of reality, logic, morality, or science. Non-Christian worldviews ultimately lead to epistemological and moral chaos.

The Transcendental Argument: The existence of the Christian God is the precondition for intelligibility. This argument asserts that the preconditions for the intelligibility of human experience are only met if the God of the Bible exists.

Antithesis: There is a fundamental opposition between Christian and non-Christian worldviews. This means there can be no neutral ground; one must either be for Christ or against Him.

The Noetic Effects of Sin: Sin affects the mind and understanding, leading to a suppression of the truth. Unbelievers are not merely neutral or ignorant of God but actively suppress the truth of God's existence and authority.

Point of Contact: Although there is antithesis between believers and unbelievers, there is also a point of contact because all people are created in the image of God and live in God's world. This common ground can be used to show unbelievers the inconsistency of their worldview.

Worldview Analysis: Apologetics involves analyzing and critiquing the presuppositions of other worldviews, demonstrating their internal inconsistencies and inability to provide a coherent basis for knowledge, ethics, or meaning.

Biblical Epistemology: True knowledge begins with the fear of the Lord. Human knowledge is dependent and derivative, relying on God's revelation rather than autonomous human reason.

Covenantal Apologetics: The relationship between God and humanity is covenantal. This involves understanding apologetics within the framework of God's redemptive history and covenantal dealings with humanity.

Holistic Approach: Apologetics is not just intellectual but also involves addressing the whole person, including their ethical, spiritual, and emotional aspects.
