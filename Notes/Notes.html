Various Readings

Leadership

John C. Maxwell's "The 21 Irrefutable Laws of Leadership" is a well-known guide that outlines essential principles for effective leadership. "How to Talk to Anyone: 92 Little Tricks for Big Success in Relationships" by Leil Lowndes provides practical tips for improving communication skills and building relationships which can be paired with those leadership qualities.

"12 Rules for Life: An Antidote to Chaos" by Jordan B. Peterson combines practical advice with philosophical insights, encouraging individuals to live more meaningful and responsible lives. "Never Split the Difference: Negotiating As If Your Life Depended On It" by Chris Voss provides strategies for successful negotiation based on the author's experience as an FBI hostage negotiator, which emphasizes the importance of psychological tactics, empathy, and preparation in negotiation to achieve better outcomes. Those skills are important in taking responsibility for your life.

"The 48 Laws of Power" by Robert Greene provides a list of principles with many examples for gaining and maintaining power in various social and professional contexts. Many of these can be mapped onto the shadow sides of the archetypes from "King, Warrior, Magician, Lover: Rediscovering the Archetypes of the Mature Masculine" by Robert Moore and Douglas Gillette.

Daily Life

"The 4-Hour Workweek: Escape 9-5, Live Anywhere, and Join the New Rich" by Timothy Ferriss outlines a lifestyle design strategy that challenges conventional work and retirement models. "Atomic Habits: An Easy & Proven Way to Build Good Habits & Break Bad Ones" by James Clear outlines several principles for habit formation and behavior change. These habits can be applied to make changes in your daily workweek to design a life where work serves your broader life goals rather than dominating them.

Philosophy

"Philosophy 101 by Socrates: An Introduction to Philosophy via Plato's Apology" by Peter Kreeft lays out 40 things philsophy is which reflect the diverse and comprehensive nature of philosophy.

Relationships

"Flirtology: Stop Swiping, Start Talking and Find Love" by Jean Smith offers practical advice on how to engage in meaningful conversations and connections, particularly in the context of finding love. "The Art of Witty Banter: Be Clever, Quick, & Magnetic" by Patrick King offers techniques and principles to improve conversational skills, making interactions more engaging and enjoyable. Together these books provide guidance to becoming a likeable, witty and outgoing person.

Teaching

"Teach Like a Champion 2.0: 62 Techniques that Put Students on the Path to College" by Doug Lemov provides practical strategies for effective teaching.

Economics and Investing

"Basic Economics: A Common Sense Guide to the Economy" by Thomas Sowell is a comprehensive overview of economic principles and concepts. "The Intelligent Investor Rev Ed.: The Definitive Book on Value Investing" by Benjamin Graham is a seminal book on value investing, offering principles that help investors make informed decisions and manage their investments wisely. By understanding principles from these books, we can make more informed and rational decisions personally and societally, ultimately achieving better long-term results while minimizing risk.

Talking Points

"Ten Global Trends Every Smart Person Should Know" by Ronald Bailey and Marian L. Tupy provides positive, measurable trends that can help counteract pessimism and foster a more informed and optimistic view of the future.
