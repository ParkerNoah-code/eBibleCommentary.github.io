The End for which God Created the World

Defining Terms: To avoid all confusion in our inquiries concerning the end for which God created the world, a distinction should be observed between the chief end for which an agent performs any work, and the ultimate end.

Reason: What may, and what may not, be supposed to be God’s ultimate end in the creation of the world? It would be relying too much on reason, to determine the affair of God’s last end in the creation of the world, without being principally guided by divine revelation. Nevertheless, as objections have been made against what the Scriptures have truly revealed, we may soberly consider in a few things, what seems rational to be supposed concerning this affair, before we proceed to consider what light divine revelation gives us in it.
