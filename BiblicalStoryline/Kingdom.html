Kingdom

Definition

"The kingdom is God’s reign through God’s people over God’s place."1

God's Reign

The vision of this kingdom isn't a utopian fantasy but rather an envisioning of a world that is structured according to God's divine grace and authority. God is the King who reigns on high!

God's People

God governs his people, but he also governs through his people. "Their relationship with God highlights the fact that they are thinking, responsible, communicative persons, and it is this relationship with God that allows them to act as God’s representative on earth."2

Sin damaged humanity's connection with God and tainted God's creation. Nevertheless, God didn't abandon his plan for his kingdom. He will reestablish his rule over the entire creation, and at the core of the kingdom's arrival lies the reconciliation of the King's followers.

God's Place

The concept of God's realm, often referred to as the kingdom, envisions His rule on earth mirroring that in heaven. This divine vision initially took root in a garden, yet its ultimate purpose was to extend globally, where individuals from diverse backgrounds and cultures would thrive under God's benevolent rule.3 While the notion of saving souls is accurate, it is not the entirety of God's plan. God, as a magnificent King, has pledged to bring about the renewal of all aspects of creation.
