Biblical - John Calvin was a 16th-century French theologian and reformer who played a crucial role in the Protestant Reformation. Calvin's theological work, especially his seminal book Institutes of the Christian Religion, laid the foundations of Reformed theology, emphasizing the sovereignty of God, predestination, and the authority of Scripture. He also wrote significant commentaries on nearly every book of the Bible.
