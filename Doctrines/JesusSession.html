Jesus Reigns in Heaven

The Heavenly Session of Christ

The present reign of Jesus Christ in heaven, commonly called His "heavenly session," is foundational to understanding His role as Lord and mediator. The term "session," derived from the Latin *sessio* (meaning "sitting"), refers to Jesus’ position at the right hand of the Father, as described in Hebrews 1:3: "After he had provided purification for sins, he sat down at the right hand of the Majesty in heaven."

While Jesus is sometimes portrayed as standing, walking among His people, or riding to battle (Acts 7:56; Rev. 2:1; 19:11–16), the image of His sitting signifies authority rather than inactivity. It reflects His sovereign reign over all creation, accomplished through His redemptive work and ascension.

Christ as King and Priest

Psalm 110 provides a foundational picture of the Messiah’s dual role as king and priest. The psalm proclaims that God has set the Messiah at His right hand to rule as king, subduing all enemies, and to serve as priest, channeling God’s grace eternally (vv. 1, 4). The New Testament applies this imagery to Jesus Christ, highlighting His ongoing reign since the Ascension (Acts 2:34–35; Heb. 1:13; 10:12; Matt. 22:44).

As king, Christ exercises authority over all realms, both angelic and human (Matt. 28:18; 1 Pet. 3:22). As priest, He intercedes for His people and ensures the application of His redemptive work. This dual role guarantees that Jesus not only rules but also ministers grace and mercy to His followers.

The Kingdom of Christ

Christ’s kingdom is manifest most directly in the church, which He heads as His body (Eph. 1:22–23). He governs the church by His Word and Spirit, guiding it in truth and empowering it for service. Unlike the Old Testament kingdom, which involved theocratic rule, the New Testament church operates without the sword. Jesus declared, "My kingdom is not of this world" (John 18:36), emphasizing that His rule is spiritual rather than enforced by earthly power.

Nevertheless, Christ also reigns over secular authorities, using them to maintain civil peace and order. While the church and state function in separate spheres, believers are called to submit to governing authorities (Rom. 13:1–7) and to live as witnesses to Christ’s lordship in every area of life. This dual reality reminds Christians that all people, whether rulers or citizens, will ultimately answer to Christ as Judge (Matt. 25:31; Acts 17:31).

Christ’s Intercession and Final Victory

Jesus’ session at the Father’s right hand includes His role as intercessor. As the reigning Lord, He continually intervenes on behalf of His people, ensuring that the benefits of His redemptive work are applied to them. His intercession is not mere supplication but an authoritative distribution of grace, made possible by His completed sacrifice (Rom. 8:34; Heb. 7:25).

This heavenly ministry will continue until all enemies are defeated, including death, the last enemy. At His return, Christ will raise the dead for judgment (John 5:28–29) and deliver the kingdom to the Father, bringing the mediatorial kingdom to its consummation (1 Cor. 15:24–28).

Encouragement for Believers

The knowledge of Christ’s session offers profound encouragement to believers. It assures them that their Lord reigns with absolute authority and intercedes for them continually. His reign guarantees that no enemy, not even death, can ultimately prevail. Christians are reminded that their King is not only sovereign but also deeply involved in their lives, guiding, empowering, and sustaining them in every circumstance.

Furthermore, Christ’s session calls believers to live under His lordship, acknowledging Him as Judge and seeking to do His will in every sphere of life. His reign is a reminder that history is moving toward a climactic fulfillment when all things will be brought under His perfect rule.

Conclusion

The heavenly session of Jesus Christ highlights His exalted position as King, Priest, and Judge. From His throne, He reigns over all creation, intercedes for His people, and directs history toward its ultimate culmination. Believers find comfort and motivation in the assurance of Christ’s sovereign rule, living in anticipation of the day when His kingdom will be fully realized, and all things will be made new.
