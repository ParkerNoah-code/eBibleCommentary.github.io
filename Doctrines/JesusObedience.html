Obedience

Jesus Christ Fulfilled His Father’s Redemptive Will

Jesus explained His relationship to the Father in terms of complete obedience: "I tell you the truth, the Son can do nothing by himself; he can do only what he sees his Father doing, because whatever the Father does the Son also does" (John 5:19). This statement reflects the perfect unity and submission within the Trinity, as Jesus carried out the Father’s redemptive plan with unwavering faithfulness.

Humility and Divine Purpose

In Scripture, humility does not imply self-deprecation but rather a faithful adherence to God’s ordained role. Jesus exemplified this humility by fully embracing the Father’s will for His life and mission (Matt. 11:29). As the second Person of the Trinity, Jesus possessed equal divinity with the Father and the Holy Spirit, yet He willingly subordinated Himself to fulfill the Father’s purpose.

The Trinitarian relationship involves cooperative unity, with the Son acting as the Father’s executive and the Spirit as the agent of both. For Jesus, it was both natural and joyful to do the Father’s will (John 4:34). This humility and obedience reflect the eternal covenant of redemption, in which the Son agreed to redeem humanity and glorify the Father through His saving work.

The Covenant of Redemption

The covenant of redemption is an eternal agreement between the Father and the Son, whereby the Father appointed Jesus as the Mediator between God and humanity. The Westminster Confession summarizes this divine purpose: Jesus was chosen to be Prophet, Priest, and King, to redeem, call, justify, sanctify, and glorify His people (Eph. 3:11; 1 Pet. 1:20; Heb. 5:5–6; Rom. 8:29–30).

This covenant unfolded in two stages: humiliation and exaltation. In His humiliation, Jesus set aside His glory to take on human nature, live as a poor man, and die as a condemned criminal bearing the sins of the world (Phil. 2:6–8; 2 Cor. 8:9; Gal. 3:13). In His exaltation, He rose from the dead, ascended to the Father, and now reigns as King over the church and the world (Phil. 2:9–11). Through His obedience, Jesus accomplished the redemption of humanity and now applies its benefits to His followers.

The Work of Christ in Exaltation

After His ascension, Christ sent the Holy Spirit to apply the redemption He secured through His death (John 15:26; Acts 2:33). He continues His redemptive work by drawing believers to Himself (John 12:32), interceding for them before the Father (Rom. 8:34; Heb. 7:25), and shepherding them through their spiritual journey (John 10:27–30).

Christ’s reign will persist until every one of God’s elect has been brought to repentance and new life (2 Pet. 3:9). In doing so, He fulfills the Father’s plan to bring many sons and daughters to glory (Heb. 2:10), demonstrating the power and effectiveness of His obedient sacrifice.

Jesus’ Joyful Obedience

Jesus’ obedience to the Father was not burdensome but a source of joy, reflecting the unity and love within the Trinity. His voluntary and joyful subordination ensures that His redemptive work glorifies both Himself and the Father. As John 5:19–23 highlights, the Father’s aim is to have the Son worshiped and glorified equally with Himself, a goal that is steadily being realized through Christ’s ongoing work.

Conclusion

Jesus Christ’s obedience to the Father is central to His redemptive mission. By willingly humbling Himself and fulfilling the Father’s plan, He accomplished the salvation of humanity and secured His exaltation as King and Savior. His example calls believers to a life of humility, obedience, and trust in God’s sovereign plan, confident in the ultimate victory of Christ’s redeeming work.
