A Christian Lives to Please God

The Calling to Glorify God

It is a central tenet of the Christian faith that every believer’s ultimate purpose is to glorify God. This calling shapes every aspect of the Christian life—how we speak, what we do, how we interact with others, and how we use the gifts and opportunities that God has given us. Every relationship, every action, every decision must be made with the intention to honor and praise God for His goodness and grace. As 1 Corinthians 10:31 instructs, "So whether you eat or drink or whatever you do, do it all for the glory of God." Similarly, Matthew 5:16 and Colossians 3:17 emphasize that our actions should point others toward God’s glory, serving as a testimony of His love and power.

This calling to glorify God is not optional or secondary for Christians; it is foundational to the very essence of the Christian life. We are to live in such a way that everything we do—whether in times of prosperity or hardship—reflects God’s goodness and draws attention to His greatness. Our lives are meant to be a continual act of worship, glorifying God in all things.

The Christian's Full-Time Employment: To Please God

Equally significant is the truth that every Christian’s personal calling is to please God in everything. Just as Jesus lived to please His Father, Christians are called to follow His example. John 8:29 and Romans 15:1–3 both affirm that Jesus did not seek to please Himself, but rather to do the will of the Father. In the same way, believers are called to live with the singular aim of pleasing God in every aspect of their lives. This involves not only outward obedience but also a heart that desires to honor and please God.

Pleasing God requires a life marked by faith (Heb. 11:5–6), praise (Ps. 69:30–31), generosity (Phil. 4:18; Heb. 13:16), obedience to authority (Col. 3:20), and wholehearted service (2 Tim. 2:4). This lifestyle is not self-generated; it is empowered by God’s grace. Hebrews 13:21 and Philippians 2:12–13 teach that God both commands and enables the believer to live in a way that pleases Him, delighting in the results of His sovereign work in us.

Godliness: Relational and Creative

True godliness is relational—it involves a deep, personal relationship with God, who is not only our Father but also our Friend. In the Bible, Abraham is called a friend of God (2 Chron. 20:7; Isa. 41:8; James 2:23), and Jesus calls His disciples friends (John 15:14). The Christian life is, at its core, a love affair with God, and godliness is about expressing that love by seeking to please Him, just as a spouse seeks to please their partner out of love. The measure of godliness is not found in mere obedience but in a heart that longs to please God in all things.

Creativity, being part of God’s image in humanity, is also an essential aspect of this relationship. Just as God is the Creator, He calls His people to express their gratitude to Him in creative ways, actively looking for opportunities to serve and please Him. Love for God naturally leads to a desire to give more, serve more, and be more generous, looking for ways to bless others and reflect God's love.

Enterprise in the Christian Life

A Christian’s life is one of enterprise—taking initiative and being proactive in showing gratitude to God through action. This enterprise involves thinking about how we can serve God more effectively, how we can bless others, and how we can live in a way that honors Him. Love will always ask, "Can I do more to please God? Can I show more love, more service, more generosity?" This mindset shapes the Christian’s approach to all aspects of life, making service to God and others central to their daily actions.

Furthermore, if our plans for pleasing God involve taking risks, we can take comfort in the parable of the talents. In this story, Jesus commends those who risked their resources to do something with them, while condemning the one who buried his talent in fear of failure (Matt. 25:14–30). Christian enterprise is not marked by fear or inaction but by a willingness to step out in faith, trusting God with the results.

Conclusion: Living to Please God

The Christian life is a life lived with the ultimate goal of pleasing God in everything. This is our calling, both in a general sense as those who glorify God with all our actions and in a personal sense as we strive to please Him in all the details of our daily lives. Pleasing God is not just about obedience but about a relationship—loving God as our Father and Friend, seeking to express our gratitude through creative and active service. Christians are called to live with purpose, looking for ways to serve others and show God's love, with an attitude of faith, courage, and risk-taking, knowing that God delights in our efforts to please Him.
