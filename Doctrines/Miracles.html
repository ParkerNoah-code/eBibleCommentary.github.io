God Shows His Presence and Power

The Biblical Concept of Miracles

In Scripture, the concept of a miracle is conveyed through three primary terms: “wonder,” “mighty work,” and “sign.” Each term highlights a unique aspect of what miracles reveal about God’s activity in the world. While there is no single biblical word for “miracle,” the Latin term miraculum captures the essence of the first concept—a wonder that evokes awe and awareness of God’s presence and power.

A miracle is an event that defies ordinary explanation, pointing instead to the direct intervention of God. Whether through striking providences, extraordinary coincidences, or acts of divine creative power, miracles awaken a heightened sense of the divine. This includes events like the raising of the dead or supernatural healings, as well as moments in everyday life that manifest God’s hand, such as childbirth or the resolution of seemingly impossible circumstances. Such wonders testify to God’s continued activity in creation and His intimate involvement with humanity.

Miracles as Mighty Works

The term “mighty work” underscores the power of God displayed in miracles. Throughout biblical history, miracles demonstrate the supernatural authority of the Creator who made the world from nothing. Events like the raising of the dead—performed by Elijah, Elisha, Jesus, Peter, and Paul—are prime examples of this divine power. These acts, which include Jesus’ resurrection, defy natural processes and cannot be explained by coincidence or the normal course of nature.

Organic healings, another common category of miracles in the Gospels, similarly showcase God’s power. These include restoring sight to the blind, cleansing lepers, and enabling the lame to walk. Such works serve not only as acts of compassion but also as demonstrations of God’s ability to restore and recreate. They remind us that God’s creative power is not bound by human limitations and that He is sovereign over life, death, and all aspects of creation.

Miracles as Signs

Miracles in Scripture often function as “signs,” conveying a specific message about God’s nature, purposes, or the identity of His representatives. In John’s Gospel, miracles are regularly referred to as signs, and the seven key miracles recorded there underscore Jesus’ divine identity and mission. For instance, the feeding of the five thousand and the raising of Lazarus reveal Jesus’ power over creation and death, affirming Him as the Son of God (John 6:1–14; 11:38–44).

Miracles authenticate the individuals who perform them, marking them as God’s chosen messengers. Moses’ miracles before Pharaoh, Elijah’s raising of the widow’s son, and Jesus’ works of healing and resurrection all serve to confirm their divine authority (Exod. 4:1–9; 1 Kings 17:24; John 10:38). Miracles also point to broader truths about God’s salvation and judgment. They reveal His power to save, His compassion for the suffering, and His sovereignty over all creation.

The Necessity of Belief in Miracles

Belief in miracles is foundational to the Christian faith. The Incarnation and Resurrection, the two supreme miracles of Scripture, are central to the gospel message. To reject miracles is to undermine the core of Christianity. Some modern theologians and scientists have dismissed miracles, not based on scientific evidence but on the philosophical assumption of a universe governed by absolute uniformity. Such a worldview denies the possibility of divine intervention.

However, there is nothing irrational about believing that the God who created the universe can intervene in it. Indeed, doubt in the miraculous is less reasonable than faith, as it requires ignoring the consistent testimony of Scripture, the witness of the church, and the experiences of countless believers who have encountered God’s miraculous power. Faith in miracles acknowledges that God’s presence and power continue to operate in the world.

Miracles Today

While the cluster of miracles recorded in Scripture is unique, their purpose remains relevant. Striking providences, extraordinary coincidences, and answered prayers often serve as modern reminders of God’s presence and activity. While not every event may qualify as a biblical “mighty work,” they still evoke wonder and point to the divine.

Christians are encouraged to approach life with an openness to God’s miraculous intervention, trusting in His power to work in ways that defy human understanding. Whether through dramatic acts of healing or the quiet workings of providence, God continues to reveal His presence and power in the world. Such moments invite believers to respond in worship, gratitude, and renewed faith.
