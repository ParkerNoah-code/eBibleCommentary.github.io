Christians Are in Society to Serve and Transform It

Understanding the Role of Christians in the World

In Colossians 2:20–22, Paul asks why Christians would submit to the principles of the world when they have died with Christ: “Since you died with Christ to the basic principles of this world, why, as though you still belonged to it, do you submit to its rules: ‘Do not handle! Do not taste! Do not touch!’?” This verse highlights the contrast between the Christian’s new life in Christ and the worldly system that is grounded in human teachings and materialism. The world, as understood in the New Testament, often refers to a fallen humanity that is opposed to God, its values corrupted by sin. It is a system driven by pride, self-interest, and the pursuit of earthly pleasures and ambitions.

Christians, however, are called to be different from the world. While they live within society, they are not to be influenced by its selfish desires and sinful values. As Jesus prayed in John 17:18, "As you sent me into the world, I have sent them into the world." Christians are sent into the world to bear witness to God's truth, share the gospel of Christ, and serve the needs of humanity, but they are to do so without adopting the world’s materialistic mindset or its fleeting ambitions.

Christian Engagement Without Worldliness

Christians are to engage with the world in a way that reflects Christ's humility and servant leadership. They must serve the world and empathize with its struggles, but they must not be attached to its worldly goals and values. In Colossians 1:9–12 and 1 Peter 2:11, Christians are reminded that they are sojourners in this world, passing through on their way to their eternal home with God. Their focus should be on pleasing God rather than conforming to the world’s desires. Jesus also instructs His followers to be wise like the people of the world in their use of resources, but to do so with an eternal perspective, seeking heavenly rewards instead of earthly ones (Luke 16:9).

The challenge for Christians is to live in the world but not become worldly. This means rejecting materialism, self-indulgence, and the pursuit of fleeting pleasure that dominate the lives of those around them. Instead, Christians are called to live by God’s moral absolutes, practicing love, holiness, and self-control, and reflecting the character of Christ in their lives (Rom. 12:2; Eph. 4:17–24; Col. 3:5–11). There must be a clean break from the world’s value systems to live in alignment with God's will.

The Christian Mandate: Evangelism, Mercy, and Stewardship

Christians have a threefold task in the world: to evangelize, to love their neighbors through acts of mercy, and to fulfill the cultural mandate God gave at creation. First and foremost, the church’s mission is evangelism. Jesus commands His followers to make disciples of all nations, baptizing them and teaching them to obey all that He has commanded (Matt. 28:19–20; Luke 24:46–48). Every Christian is called to be a witness for Christ and to share the good news of salvation with those who have not yet believed.

In addition to evangelism, Christians are called to practice neighbor-love, actively seeking to meet the needs of others through acts of mercy and compassion (Matt. 5:16). Whether through caring for the poor, the sick, or the marginalized, Christians are to embody the love of Christ in practical ways. This service to others is a powerful testimony to the transformative power of the gospel.

Furthermore, Christians are called to fulfill the "cultural mandate" given to humanity in Genesis 1:28–30. God made humanity to steward His creation, and this responsibility is carried out in various forms of work that honor God and serve the good of others. Whether in farming, teaching, technology, or any other field, Christians are to work diligently and with integrity, seeking to glorify God through their labor. This is what underpins the Protestant "work ethic," where all work is considered sacred when done for God's glory.

Christians as Salt and Light in the World

Jesus calls His followers to be "salt" and "light" in the world (Matt. 5:13–16). Salt is a preservative, preventing decay and corruption, while light illuminates the truth and guides others. As Christians live out their faith, they become a transforming presence in society, bringing God’s truth, love, and justice to bear in a broken world. Through their words and actions, Christians are meant to reflect the light of Christ and draw others to Him.

The Christian life, when lived out according to God’s word, becomes a powerful force for cultural transformation. As Christians uphold God’s values and serve the world through acts of mercy and diligent work, they fulfill their calling to be agents of change, contributing to the world’s redemption and pointing others to the ultimate hope found in Christ.

Conclusion: Christians in the World

Christians are called to live in the world, but not to be of it. They are sent by Christ to witness, serve, and transform society, all while remaining distinct from the world’s values and systems. This calls for a life of holiness, love, and integrity, where every action is motivated by the desire to glorify God and serve others. As Christians fulfill their calling to evangelize, show mercy, and steward God’s creation, they become a powerful force for good in the world, bringing light to darkness and salt to a decaying society.
