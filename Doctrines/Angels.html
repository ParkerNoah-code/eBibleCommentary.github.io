God Employs Supernatural Agents

The Nature and Characteristics of Angels

Angels, whose name means “messengers,” are one of two types of personal beings created by God, the other being humankind. They are numerous (Matt. 26:53; Rev. 5:11) and exist as intelligent, moral agents who are not typically embodied or visible. However, they are capable of appearing to humans in forms that resemble physical bodies, as demonstrated in encounters such as those with Abraham and Mary Magdalene (Gen. 18:2–19:22; John 20:10–14).

Unlike humans, angels do not marry and are not subject to death (Matt. 22:30; Luke 20:35–36). They possess extraordinary mobility, able to traverse space and gather in great numbers within confined areas (Luke 8:30). While many angels remain in a state of holiness and glory, others, following their probationary period, fell into sin, becoming what Scripture identifies as demons or fallen angels.

The Role of Holy Angels

The holy angels, confirmed in their righteousness, dwell in heaven, where they worship God ceaselessly (Pss. 103:20–21; 148:2; Rev. 5:11). From this heavenly headquarters, they carry out God’s commands, particularly in serving believers (Heb. 1:14). They are referred to as “holy” and “elect” angels (Matt. 25:31; 1 Tim. 5:21) and are witnesses to God’s unfolding plan of grace, gaining deeper insight into His wisdom and glory through the work of salvation (Eph. 3:10; 1 Pet. 1:12).

These angels perform various ministries on behalf of God’s people. They guard believers (Pss. 34:7; 91:11) and show particular concern for children (Matt. 18:10). They observe the church’s activities (1 Cor. 11:10) and are believed to assist believers at the moment of death (Luke 16:22). Though details about these activities remain mysterious, Scripture assures believers that angelic help is available whenever needed.

The Angel of the Lord

A mysterious figure in the Old Testament, the “angel of the Lord” is often depicted as both distinct from and identical with God. This figure appears in significant moments, such as Hagar’s encounter in the wilderness (Gen. 16:7–13), the burning bush (Exod. 3:2–6), and Joshua’s meeting with the commander of the Lord’s army (Josh. 5:13–15). Many theologians view these appearances as preincarnate manifestations of God the Son, acting as His own messenger.

The presence of the angel of the Lord highlights God’s direct involvement in the lives of His people. These encounters demonstrate God’s power, guidance, and care, preparing the way for the fuller revelation of Christ in the New Testament.

Angels in Salvation History

Angelic activity is particularly prominent at key turning points in God’s redemptive plan. During the patriarchal era, angels communicated God’s promises and guidance. At the time of the Exodus, they played roles in delivering and protecting Israel. Angels also ministered during the Exile and restoration periods, providing visions and messages to prophets like Daniel and Zechariah.

In the New Testament, angels announce the birth of Christ (Luke 2:9–14), minister to Him during His temptation (Matt. 4:11), and proclaim His resurrection (Matt. 28:5–7). They are also present at His ascension (Acts 1:10–11) and will accompany Him at His return in glory (Matt. 25:31; Mark 8:38). These appearances underscore the role of angels as supernatural agents who reveal God’s purposes and demonstrate His power and presence.

The Relevance of Angels Today

While angelic activity may seem less visible in the modern world, their ministry remains active. Believers can trust that angels continue to serve God’s purposes, protecting, guiding, and ministering to those who inherit salvation. Their example of worship and obedience also serves as a model for Christians, encouraging greater devotion to God.

The knowledge of angelic activity provides comfort and assurance to believers. It reminds them that they are not alone in their spiritual journey and that God employs supernatural agents to accomplish His will. As Scripture teaches, the presence and ministry of angels are an integral part of God’s providential care for His people.
