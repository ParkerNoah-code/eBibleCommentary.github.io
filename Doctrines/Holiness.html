God Is Light

The Meaning of God’s Holiness

The term "holy," as applied to God in scripture, signifies the divine quality that sets Him apart from all creation, evoking awe, adoration, and reverence. Leviticus 11:44 commands, “Be holy, because I am holy,” emphasizing God’s purity and perfection as the standard for His people. This holiness is an essential attribute that touches every facet of God’s being, encompassing His moral perfection and transcendent greatness.

God’s holiness is foundational to His other attributes. His love is holy love; His justice is holy justice; His mercy is holy mercy. This purity not only defines His character but also underscores His intolerance for sin (Hab. 1:13). When confronted with God’s holiness, humanity is compelled to acknowledge its unworthiness, as seen in Isaiah’s cry of despair in Isaiah 6:5: “Woe to me!... I am a man of unclean lips.”

Holiness and Divine Justice

One of the most evident expressions of God’s holiness is His justice. Justice, as an outworking of holiness, requires that God act righteously in all circumstances. His moral law, described as “holy, righteous, and good” (Rom. 7:12), reflects His pure nature and sets the standard for human conduct. As both lawgiver and judge, God administers justice according to what is right and true (Gen. 18:25; Acts 17:31).

God’s justice is further displayed in His wrath against sin. Far from being arbitrary, His wrath is a holy response to evil, an expression of His unwavering opposition to all that contradicts His nature (Rom. 2:5–16). Yet, this justice is tempered by His covenant faithfulness. His acts of salvation, such as redeeming Israel or justifying sinners through faith in Christ, reveal that His justice and mercy are perfectly intertwined (Isa. 51:5–6; Rom. 3:25–26). This dual demonstration of justice magnifies His holiness, as He both judges sin and offers redemption.

“God Is Light”: Holiness and Purity

John’s declaration that “God is light; in him there is no darkness at all” (1 John 1:5) reinforces the centrality of God’s holiness. Light here symbolizes absolute purity and moral integrity, emphasizing that God’s holiness is untainted by any imperfection. This purity renders fellowship between God and unrepentant sinners impossible, demanding a response of repentance and pursuit of righteousness (1 John 1:6–7; 2 Cor. 6:14–7:1).

The call for holiness extends to God’s people. Just as God’s light reveals and eradicates darkness, His holiness demands transformation in the lives of believers. Hebrews 12:10–17 describes holiness as essential for seeing the Lord, while passages like 1 Peter 1:15–16 echo the Levitical command: “Be holy, because I am holy.”

The Pursuit of Holiness in God’s People

Holiness is not merely an attribute of God but a standard for His people. From the Old Testament to the New, believers are summoned to mirror God’s character in their lives. Deuteronomy 30:1–10 outlines the blessings of covenantal obedience, while passages like Ephesians 4:17–5:14 describe the process of putting off sinful behaviors and embracing a life reflective of God’s nature.

The New Testament places particular emphasis on holiness as an integral part of salvation. Regeneration through Christ enables believers to pursue the holiness that pleases God (John 3:1–8; 1 John 1:9). This pursuit is not optional but central to the Christian life, shaping every thought, word, and deed in alignment with God’s will.

Practical Implications of God’s Holiness

God’s holiness serves as both a comfort and a challenge. It assures believers of His unchanging purity and justice, making Him a reliable refuge in a world marked by corruption. Simultaneously, it calls them to continual growth in righteousness, reflecting His light in a darkened world (Matt. 5:14–16).

In worship, God’s holiness inspires reverence and humility. As Isaiah experienced in his vision (Isa. 6:1–8), encountering God’s holiness compels self-examination and leads to deeper dependence on His grace. It also strengthens the believer’s hope, as the holy God who calls His people to holiness is also the one who empowers them to achieve it through His Spirit.

Ultimately, God’s holiness is not merely a doctrine to be understood but a reality to be lived. It transforms lives, aligning them with the purity and righteousness of the God who is light.
