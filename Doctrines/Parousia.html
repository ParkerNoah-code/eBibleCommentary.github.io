The Return of Jesus Christ

The Certainty of Christ’s Return

The New Testament repeatedly affirms the truth that Jesus Christ will return to the earth in glory. This event, known as the Second Coming or "parousia" in Greek, will mark the culmination of human history. Jesus will return personally and physically, in a visible and triumphant manner (Matt. 24:44; Acts 1:11; Col. 3:4; 2 Tim. 4:8). His return will be a moment of divine intervention to end the present age and inaugurate the final stage of God's redemptive plan. It will be an event that not only completes history but also brings the resurrection of the dead and the final judgment of all people (John 5:28–29).

Christ’s return will be both an ending and a beginning. It will mark the end of the current world order, where sin, suffering, and death have their sway. It will be a time of judgment and of final glory for God's people, as they receive the fulfillment of their inheritance (Rom. 8:17–18; Col. 3:4). Furthermore, it will usher in a new heaven and new earth, where all things will be made new and perfect (Rom. 8:19–21; 2 Pet. 3:10–13). Christ’s return will thus bring about the completion of God's kingdom, the defeat of Satan, and the establishment of eternal peace.

The Role of Christ in the Final Judgment

At His return, Christ will judge the living and the dead. This event will be a final reckoning where all people will give an account for their lives. Those who have trusted in Christ will be raised to eternal glory, while those who have rejected Him will face eternal separation from God. The return of Christ is not merely a time of personal victory but also a time of cosmic renewal, where the entire created order will be transformed (Rom. 8:19–21). As Paul explains in 1 Corinthians 15:24–28, after Christ’s triumph over all enemies, including death, He will hand over the kingdom to the Father, signaling the completion of God's redemptive plan.

The culmination of Christ's work will not diminish His honor or authority. Instead, it will serve as the final confirmation of His victory and the eternal reign of God. In the new Jerusalem, God and the Lamb will reign together forever (Rev. 22:1, 3), in a continuous and unbroken relationship with the redeemed. This eternal reign is not the continuation of Christ’s mediatorial kingdom but the fulfillment of God's eternal purpose for His creation.

The Description of Christ’s Return

The return of Christ will be a dramatic event, described in 1 Thessalonians 4:16–17 as a descent from the sky, accompanied by the sound of a trumpet, a shout, and the voice of the archangel. This event will be marked by the resurrection of the dead in Christ, who will rise first, and the "rapture" of the living believers, who will be caught up to meet Christ in the air. This momentous event will serve as the triumphant return of Christ, with His people accompanying Him as He descends to earth to establish His reign. The concept of a "third coming" or an additional period before Christ’s final return lacks support in Scripture.

The trumpet, reminiscent of military or royal proclamations (Exod. 19:16, 19; Isa. 27:13), signals the beginning of God’s climactic intervention in history. The clouds in which Christ will appear symbolize God's presence, as seen in Old Testament events (Exod. 19:9, 16; Dan. 7:13; Matt. 24:30). While these symbols carry deeper theological significance, the description should be understood as a literal portrayal of the event, even if it is beyond human comprehension. This moment will mark the fulfillment of God's promises, with Christ's return signaling the ultimate victory over sin and death.

Uncertainty of the Timing of Christ’s Return

The New Testament makes it clear that the exact timing of Christ's return is unknown. Jesus Himself said that no one knows the day or hour, not even the angels in heaven, but only the Father (Matt. 24:36–51). Despite numerous predictions and signs, including the fall of Jerusalem in A.D. 70 (Luke 21:20, 24), the timing of Christ’s return remains a mystery. Jesus' return will come unexpectedly, like a thief in the night (1 Thess. 5:1–4), and those who are unprepared will face disaster. The uncertainty of the timing is meant to encourage vigilance and readiness among believers.

The events leading up to Christ’s return will include the spread of the gospel (Matt. 24:14), apostasy and tribulation (2 Thess. 2:3; 1 Tim. 4:1), and the rise of false prophets and antichrists (Matt. 24:5, 24; 1 John 2:18). These signs are not meant to allow believers to calculate a specific date for Christ's return but to serve as reminders to remain faithful and watchful. The key is not knowing the exact time but living in a state of preparedness, continually serving God and anticipating Christ’s return at any moment.

The Significance of Christ’s Return for Believers

For believers, the return of Christ marks the end of life in this world and the beginning of a new life in God’s presence. Just as death brings the Christian into a new reality with Christ, so too will the return of Christ be the ultimate realization of all that believers have hoped for. It will bring about the full restoration of creation and the final defeat of all evil. Christians are called to live with this hope, staying alert and prepared for Christ’s return at any moment (Matt. 25:1–13). The thought of Christ's return should encourage believers to persevere in faith, live godly lives, and remain steadfast in their service to Christ until the end (1 Cor. 15:58).
