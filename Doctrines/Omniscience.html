God Sees and Knows

The Scope of God's Knowledge

Proverbs 15:3 declares, “The eyes of the LORD are everywhere, keeping watch on the wicked and the good.” This verse encapsulates the doctrine of God’s omniscience, the divine attribute of knowing all things. Unlike human knowledge, which is limited and fragmented, God’s knowledge is perfect, complete, and infinite. He knows everything about everyone and everything at all times (Job 24:23; Psalm 33:13–15).

God’s omniscience extends beyond observable facts. He understands the hidden motives of human hearts (1 Samuel 16:7), the intricacies of creation (Psalm 139:13–16), and the complexities of every action and event in the world (Jeremiah 16:17). Furthermore, His knowledge is not limited to the present; He knows the past, the future, and even potential outcomes that never come to pass (1 Samuel 23:9–13; Isaiah 48:18). This boundless knowledge demonstrates God’s unmatched wisdom and intelligence (Isaiah 40:28; Romans 11:33–36).

God’s Knowledge and His Sovereignty

God’s omniscience is inseparably linked to His sovereignty. He knows all things because He created all things, sustains them, and orchestrates them according to His eternal purposes (Ephesians 1:11). Every aspect of creation operates under His control, and every detail aligns with His perfect plan.

The idea that God could fully know all things without also governing them defies both Scripture and logic. As the sovereign Creator and Sustainer, God’s comprehensive knowledge ensures that He directs all things toward His glory and the ultimate good of His creation.

The Awe of God’s Omniscience

The Bible often portrays God’s omniscience as a source of awe and reverence. In Psalm 139:1–6, the psalmist marvels at God’s intimate knowledge of his life, exclaiming, “Such knowledge is too wonderful for me, too lofty for me to attain.” Similarly, Isaiah 40:13–14 asks, “Who can fathom the Spirit of the LORD, or instruct the LORD as his counselor?” These passages remind us of the infinite capacity of God’s mind, which surpasses human understanding.

Theologians have often noted that God’s knowledge is not acquired or stored like human knowledge. Rather, it is immediate, direct, and eternal. God does not "access" information or learn new things; He knows all things instinctively and simultaneously.

The Comfort and Challenge of God’s Omniscience

For Christians, God’s omniscience provides profound comfort and assurance. Isaiah 40:27–31 encourages believers with the truth that God’s knowledge encompasses every detail of their lives, ensuring that they are not forgotten or overlooked. His infinite awareness is a promise of His care and provision, as He is fully aware of every need, struggle, and hope.

For those who reject God, however, His omniscience is a sobering reality. The truth that no thought, action, or motive is hidden from God’s sight underscores the inescapable nature of His judgment (Psalm 94:1–11). In John 1:1–12, the omniscient God is revealed as both Creator and Judge, holding all accountable for their response to His light.

Responding to God’s Omniscience

The knowledge of God’s omniscience should inspire worship, accountability, and trust. For believers, it is an invitation to live transparently before Him, knowing that nothing is hidden from His sight (Hebrews 4:13). This truth also encourages believers to rest in God’s wisdom and care, confident that He knows their needs and directs their paths with perfect understanding.

For those outside the faith, God’s omniscience serves as a call to repentance and surrender. The reality of God’s all-seeing presence challenges individuals to recognize their need for forgiveness and seek the grace offered through Jesus Christ. Ultimately, God’s omniscience reveals His holiness, justice, and love, drawing all people to respond to Him in faith and obedience.
