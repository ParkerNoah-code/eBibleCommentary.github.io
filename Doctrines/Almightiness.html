God is Omnipresent and Omnipotent

God’s Omnipresence: Present Everywhere in Fullness

Jeremiah 23:24 poses a rhetorical question from God: “Can anyone hide in secret places so that I cannot see him? … Do not I fill heaven and earth?” This affirmation highlights the omnipresence of God, whose presence permeates all creation. Unlike physical beings, God is pure spirit, existing without physical dimensions. His presence is not spatially distributed but fully manifest in all places at all times.

This divine attribute assures believers that God’s attention is never divided or diminished. Psalm 139:7–10 reflects on this truth, marveling that no place in heaven, earth, or the depths can escape God’s presence. Paul echoes this reality in Acts 17:24–28, describing God as the one in whom “we live and move and have our being.” The fullness of the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit is available everywhere, though the Son’s personal presence is mediated through the Holy Spirit.

God’s Omnipotence: Infinite Power Governed by Wisdom

Job’s declaration, “I know that you can do all things; no plan of yours can be thwarted” (Job 42:2), encapsulates the doctrine of God’s omnipotence. God’s almightiness means that He can accomplish everything He wills in perfect wisdom and goodness. However, this does not imply that God can perform logical impossibilities, such as creating a square circle, or act against His own nature. God cannot lie, sin, or contradict the moral demands of His holiness (Numbers 23:19; 2 Timothy 2:13; Hebrews 6:18).

God’s omnipotence assures us of His ability to fulfill every promise and execute every plan without obstruction. This power is not raw or arbitrary but governed by His moral perfection, aligning with His character and purposes. His omnipotence inspires confidence and trust in His providential care.

Faith Inspired by God’s Greatness

The psalmists’ expressions of love, trust, and praise for God are deeply rooted in their knowledge of His omnipresence and omnipotence. Psalm 18:1–2 exclaims, “The LORD is my rock, my fortress and my deliverer; my God is my rock, in whom I take refuge.” Similarly, Psalm 46:1 declares, “God is our refuge and strength, an ever-present help in trouble.”

These declarations reflect the natural response to the realization of God’s greatness. His omnipresence assures believers of His nearness, while His omnipotence guarantees His ability to protect and deliver. Such knowledge inspires great faith, steadfast hope, and exuberant praise, anchoring believers in the certainty of God’s sovereign care.

The Practical Implications of God’s Almightiness

God’s omnipresence and omnipotence have profound implications for the life of faith. His omnipresence means that no place or situation is devoid of His presence, providing comfort in times of loneliness or fear. Believers can approach Him in prayer, confident that He hears and attends to their needs, no matter where they are.

His omnipotence assures believers of His ability to intervene in every circumstance. Whether delivering from danger, providing strength in weakness, or fulfilling His promises, God’s power is always at work for the good of those who love Him (Romans 8:28). This assurance fosters a deep trust in His purposes and a willingness to surrender to His will.

The knowledge of God’s almightiness transforms the believer’s perspective on life. It invites us to live in awe of His greatness, to rest in His power, and to worship Him as the God who fills heaven and earth with His presence and sustains all things by His might.
