Ordinances > Baptism:

To my Pedobaptist brethren:

Will you not here pause, and with the Bible in your hand, prayerfully re-examine this whole subject? You may have never, at any time, given it a careful investigation. Many have found it in their Church, and feeling, very naturally, a prejudice in favor of whatever she approves, and observes, received and adopted it. And since then have practiced the rite under a sort of indefinite impression that, although with no clearness how, it is defensible by the word of God. This, I know, is the position occupied by thousands. Those who do not design to depart from the gospel. Least of all is it imagined by those who practice infant baptism that it would be committing an injury in any way. The enormous evil it brings upon the parents, children, the church, and the world is a great fact to which your attention needs to be called.

You may still regard it with favor because it is observed by your church; because great men practice, and defend it; because it is a time-honored practice which has come down to you through a period of fifteen centuries; and because you have thought that "If it does no good, it will do no harm!" But great men, as great, and as good, as any that have defended, and practiced infant baptism, have also defended, and practiced, all the corruptions of Popery. If on this account you receive infant baptism, you are obliged, for the same reasons, to receive all the corruptions of Popery.

That, too, is a time-honored institution, clothed with the sanction of more than twelve centuries. High position, great learning, and venerableness can never give authority to any thing which is in itself false, and injurious. Ours is neither the age, nor the country, nor is religion the subject, in which such arguments can be respected. Because our fathers were governed by kings, and emperors, who, as they were taught by great, and good, men, "ruled by divine right," shall we be monarchists? We choose in politics, to exercise our own judgment, and we reject as baseless, all these antiquated pretensions. Shall we be less wise in religion? Here, too, we will look not to men, but to God; not to antiquity, but to divine revelation. Our appeal is, "to the law, and to the testimony. If we speak not according to these, it is because there is no light in us."

List of reasons to reject infant baptism:

1. Its practice is unsupported by the word of God.

2. Its defense leads to injurious perversions of Scripture.

3. It engrafts Judaism upon the gospel of Christ.

4. It falsifies the doctrine of universal depravity.

5. The doctrines upon which it is predicated contradict the great fundamental principle of justification by faith.

6. It is in direct conflict with the doctrine of the work of the Holy Spirit in regeneration.

7. It despoils the church of those peculiar qualities which are essential to the church of Christ.

8. Its practice perpetuates the superstitions that originally produced it.

9. It subverts the scripture doctrine of infant salvation.

10. It leads its advocates into rebellion against the authority of Christ.

11. The connection it assumes with the moral and religious training of children.

12. It is the grand foundation upon which rests the union of church and state.

13. It leads to religious persecutions.

14. It is contrary to the principles of civil and religious freedom.

15. It enfeebles the power of the church to combat error.

16. It injures the credit of religion with reflecting men of the world.

17. It is the great barrier to Christian union.

18. It prevents the salutary impression which baptism was designed to make upon the minds both of those who receive it, and of those who witness its administration.

19. It retards the designs of Christ in the conversion of the world.
