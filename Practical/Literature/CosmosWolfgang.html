Wolfgang Smith, Cosmos & Transcendence: Breaking Through the Barrier of Scientistic Belief, (La Salle: Sherwood, 1984).

Objective of the Book

Smith makes an important distinction between scientific knowledge, that which is observable and emperically verifiable, and scientistic belief, that which is presupposed in science and not subject to scientific analysis. Scientific knowledge can be rigorously verified through the scientific method. It relies on tangible evidence and is continually subject to scrutiny and validation. In contrast, scientistic beliefs are not amenable to empirical verification, and they form the philosophical underpinnings of scientific endeavors.

Smith demonstrates that the many scientistic tenets upon which science rests are volatile and groundless. Looking at the fields of cosmology, evolution, and psychology, Smith traces the origin of the scientistic unconscious that has perpetuated these ideas and formed an unfounded, dogmatic confidence in science.

Finally, he considers the metaphysical concept of transcendence, "breaking through the barrier of scientistic belief." Having revealed the limitations of emperical science and scientistic belief, he invites us to see an enlarged picture of the universe and of what lies beyond it.
