C. Christ is the Center (2:16-19)

In union with Him we have once and forever put away the sins of the flesh, have lain in His grave, have passed to the heavenside of death, and are living under the blue sky of acceptance with God. Our Master’s victory is ours through faith. He won it, but we may share its fruits. By faith we apprehend and affirm these blessings. The land of Canaan is ours by right, but every inch has to be claimed by faith.

We must not allow our religious life to become a piece of outward ritual, Col 2:16-17; nor permit the supposed mediation of angels to obscure the supreme majesty of our Lord, Col 2:18-19.

Ver 16-17. Let no one therefore judge you in eating or drinking, or with respect to a feast day or a new moon or a Sabbath day, which are a shadow of the things to come; but the body is Christ’s.

Ver 18. Let no one rob you of your prize by self-abasement and worshiping of the angels, dwelling in the things which he has not seen, vainly puffed up by his fleshly mind,

Ver 19. and not holding firmly to the Head, from whom all the body, being supplied and knit together through the joints and ligaments, grows with God’s growth.
