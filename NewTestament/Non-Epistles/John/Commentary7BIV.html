Commentary:

The Vine and the Branches (15:1-17)

Jesus’ discourse on the vine and the branches, the believer and the world.

Summary

15:1-10
Jesus is the true vine, in which the life of believers, as abiding branches, is hid. Abiding in Him, the believer is able to walk in purity of life, with Christ sharing in all the interests of his life, taking all burdens to Him, and drawing all wisdom and strength from Him. It is only the abiding life that bears fruit to the glory of the Father.

15:9-11

15:12-17
Christ proved His love to the world by laying down His life for it (1 Jn 3:16) in redemption work. His love to us should ever be the standard of measure of our love to others in service for Him.

Old Testament

In the Old Testament the vine is the type of Israel, planted by the Almighty as the husbandman to adorn, refresh, and quicken the earth (Psalms 80; Isa 5:1; 27:2; Jer 2:21; Ezek 19:10; Hos 10:1). But Israel proved itself ‘the degenerate plant of a strange vine’ (Jer 2:21). Jesus, therefore, is here the ‘true vine,’ because He is the true Israel of God, in whom is fulfilled all that is demanded of the true vine, whether for blessing to the world, or for glory to the husbandman (vine-dresser).

Union with Christ

1. The relation of the disciples to Christ is one of living union as branches in the vine (Jn 15:1-11).

2. The result of this union in the lives of the disciples is love (Jn 15:12-17).

3. And union with Chirst in the midst of an unbelieving world (John 15:18-27).

Ver 1. I am the true vine, and my Father is the farmer.

Ver 2. Every branch in me that doesn’t bear fruit, he takes away. Every branch that bears fruit, he prunes, that it may bear more fruit.

Ver 3. You are already pruned clean because of the word which I have spoken to you.

Ver 4. Remain in me, and I in you. As the branch can’t bear fruit by itself unless it remains in the vine, so neither can you, unless you remain in me.

Ver 5. I am the vine. You are the branches. He who remains in me and I in him bears much fruit, for apart from me you can do nothing.

Ver 6. If a man doesn’t remain in me, he is thrown out as a branch and is withered; and they gather them, throw them into the fire, and they are burned.

Ver 7. If you remain in me, and my words remain in you, you will ask whatever you desire, and it will be done for you.

Ver 8. In this my Father is glorified, that you bear much fruit; and so you will be my disciples.

Ver 9. Even as the Father has loved me, I also have loved you. Remain in my love.

Ver 10. If you keep my commandments, you will remain in my love, even as I have kept my Father’s commandments and remain in his love.

Ver 11. I have spoken these things to you, that my joy may remain in you, and that your joy may be made full.

Ver 12. This is my commandment, that you love one another, even as I have loved you.

Ver 13. Greater love has no one than this, that someone lay down his life for his friends.

Ver 14. You are my friends if you do whatever I command you.

Ver 15. No longer do I call you servants, for the servant doesn’t know what his lord does. But I have called you friends, for everything that I heard from my Father, I have made known to you.

Ver 16. You didn’t choose me, but I chose you and appointed you, that you should go and bear fruit, and that your fruit should remain; that whatever you will ask of the Father in my name, he may give it to you.

Ver 17. I command these things to you, that you may love one another.
