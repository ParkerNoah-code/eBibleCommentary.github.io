A. Problem: Taming the Tongue (3:1-12)

Ver 1. Let not many of you be teachers, my brothers, knowing that we will receive heavier judgment.

Ver 2. For we all stumble in many things. Anyone who doesn’t stumble in word is a perfect person, able to bridle the whole body also.

Ver 3. Indeed, we put bits into the horses’ mouths so that they may obey us, and we guide their whole body.

Ver 4. Behold, the ships also, though they are so big and are driven by fierce winds, are yet guided by a very small rudder, wherever the pilot desires.

Ver 5. So the tongue is also a little member, and boasts great things. See how a small fire can spread to a large forest!

Ver 6. And the tongue is a fire. The world of iniquity among our members is the tongue, which defiles the whole body, and sets on fire the course of nature, and is set on fire by Gehenna.

Ver 7. For every kind of animal, bird, creeping thing, and sea creature is tamed, and has been tamed by mankind;

Ver 8. but nobody can tame the tongue. It is a restless evil, full of deadly poison.

Ver 9. With it we bless our God and Father, and with it we curse men who are made in the image of God.

Ver 10. Out of the same mouth comes blessing and cursing. My brothers, these things ought not to be so.

Ver 11. Does a spring send out from the same opening fresh and bitter water?

Ver 12. Can a fig tree, my brothers, yield olives, or a vine figs? Thus no spring yields both salt water and fresh water.
