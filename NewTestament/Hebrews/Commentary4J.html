J. Faith: Overcoming the World (11:32-40)

Ver 32. What more shall I say? For the time would fail me if I told of Gideon, Barak, Samson, Jephthah, David, Samuel, and the prophets—

Ver 33. who through faith subdued kingdoms, worked out righteousness, obtained promises, stopped the mouths of lions,

Ver 34. quenched the power of fire, escaped the edge of the sword, from weakness were made strong, grew mighty in war, and caused foreign armies to flee.

Ver 35. Women received their dead by resurrection. Others were tortured, not accepting their deliverance, that they might obtain a better resurrection.

Ver 36. Others were tried by mocking and scourging, yes, moreover by bonds and imprisonment.

Ver 37. They were stoned. They were sawn apart. They were tempted. They were slain with the sword. They went around in sheep skins and in goat skins; being destitute, afflicted, ill-treated—

Ver 38. of whom the world was not worthy—wandering in deserts, mountains, caves, and the holes of the earth.

Ver 39. These all, having been commended for their faith, didn’t receive the promise,

Ver 40. God having provided some better thing concerning us, so that apart from us they should not be made perfect.
