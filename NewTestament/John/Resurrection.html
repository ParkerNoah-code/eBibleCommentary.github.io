John

IX. The Resurrection (20:1-31)

The empty tomb is a sign that Jesus has triumphed over sin and death. In the tomb lies not a lifeless body because Jesus has risen and taken on a new glorious body. Jesus has shattered the chains that held humanity captive, offering a path to salvation. His death is not just a personal victory, but a universal victory. It is the beginning of God's plan to renew and transform all of creation, healing its wounds and ushering in the kingdom of God. The resurrection declares that Jesus is the eternal and living Christ, and the Lord of all creation.

Outline

A. The Empty Tomb (20:1-10) - Jesus has completed the work he set out to accomplish and has inaugurated the restoration of all things, Christ being the first fruits.

B. Appearance to Mary Magdalene (20:11-18) - The cross shows how God works all things for good and how Jesus turns our weeping into joy.

C. Appearance to the Disciples (20:19-23) - Jesus calms our fears with the true peace of God and sends us out in the power of the Holy Spirit.

D. Appearance to Thomas (20:24-31) - The written Gospel servers to preserve the message of Jesus so that readers would believe in Christ and have life in his name.

Summary

On Sunday morning while it was still dark, Mary Magdalene went to the tomb and discovered that it was empty (1). Supposing that they had moved the body, she ran to tell Peter and John that she didn't know where they had taken him (2). After hearing the news, Peter and John ran to the tomb, which they likewise found empty, and upon walking in discovered the linen cloths and face cloth from Jesus' body lying neatly there (3-8). The disciples return home believing that Jesus had risen from the dead but not yet understanding how this fulfills the Old Testament (9-10).

Mary Magdalene, who was weeping, moved to see inside the tomb, and saw two angels in white, one where the head of the body of Jesus had been and one where his feet had been (11-12). The angels asked her why she was weeping, and she told them it was because they had taken away her Lord (13). At that moment, she turned around and saw Jesus, who she mistook to be a gardener because of his resurrected body, who asked why she was weeping and whom she was seeking; again she asked about the location of the body of Jesus (14-15). Right after that, Mary recognizes Jesus and is sent to tell the disciples that he will ascend to the Father soon, so she goes and annouces to the disciples that she has seen the Lord (17-18).

A few hours later, Jesus entered the place where the disciples were despite the locked doors (19). He showed them his hands and his side where he had been pierced and commisions them to go out and proclaim the gospel (20-21). Jesus breathes on them and tells them to recieve the Holy Spirit (22). The gospel they are to proclaim is about the forgiveness of sins (23).

Thomas refuses to believe the resurrection until Jesus appears again to the disciples and allows Thomas to touch the wounds (24-27). This leads to Thomas' great confession of his faith in Jesus: "My Lord and my God!" (28). John tells us that his purpose in writing is that we also may have faith in Jesus, believing that Jesus is the Christ, the Son of God, and that by believing we might have life in his name (29-31).

Context in John

“Destroy this temple, and in three days I will raise it up” (Jn 2:19).

"No one has ascended into heaven but he who descended out of heaven, the Son of Man, who is in heaven. As Moses lifted up the serpent in the wilderness, even so must the Son of Man be lifted up, that whoever believes in him should not perish, but have eternal life. For God so loved the world, that he gave his only born Son, that whoever believes in him should not perish, but have eternal life. For God didn’t send his Son into the world to judge the world, but that the world should be saved through him. He who believes in him is not judged. He who doesn’t believe has been judged already, because he has not believed in the name of the only born Son of God" (Jn 3:13-18).

"Don’t marvel at this, for the hour comes in which all who are in the tombs will hear his voice and will come out; those who have done good, to the resurrection of life; and those who have done evil, to the resurrection of judgment" (Jn 5:28-29).

"Even if I testify about myself, my testimony is true, for I know where I came from, and where I am going; but you don’t know where I came from, or where I am going." (Jn 8:14).

"I am the resurrection and the life. He who believes in me will still live, even if he dies." (Jn 11:25).

New Testament Resurrection

"But now Christ has been raised from the dead. He became the first fruit of those who are asleep. For since death came by man, the resurrection of the dead also came by man. For as in Adam all die, so also in Christ all will be made alive. But each in his own order: Christ the first fruits, then those who are Christ’s at his coming. Then the end comes, when he will deliver up the Kingdom to God the Father, when he will have abolished all rule and all authority and power. For he must reign until he has put all his enemies under his feet. The last enemy that will be abolished is death." (1 Cor 15:20-26).

"Therefore we are always confident and know that while we are at home in the body, we are absent from the Lord; for we walk by faith, not by sight. We are courageous, I say, and are willing rather to be absent from the body and to be at home with the Lord. Therefore also we make it our aim, whether at home or absent, to be well pleasing to him. For we must all be revealed before the judgment seat of Christ that each one may receive the things in the body according to what he has done, whether good or bad" (2 Cor 5:6-10).

"But we don’t want you to be ignorant, brothers, concerning those who have fallen asleep, so that you don’t grieve like the rest, who have no hope. For if we believe that Jesus died and rose again, even so God will bring with him those who have fallen asleep in Jesus. For this we tell you by the word of the Lord, that we who are alive, who are left until the coming of the Lord, will in no way precede those who have fallen asleep. For the Lord himself will descend from heaven with a shout, with the voice of the archangel and with God’s trumpet. The dead in Christ will rise first, then we who are alive, who are left, will be caught up together with them in the clouds to meet the Lord in the air. So we will be with the Lord forever. Therefore comfort one another with these words" (1 Thess 4:13-18).

"I saw thrones, and they sat on them, and judgment was given to them. I saw the souls of those who had been beheaded for the testimony of Jesus and for the word of God, and such as didn’t worship the beast nor his image, and didn’t receive the mark on their forehead and on their hand. They lived and reigned with Christ for a thousand years. The rest of the dead didn’t live until the thousand years were finished. This is the first resurrection. Blessed and holy is he who has part in the first resurrection. Over these, the second death has no power, but they will be priests of God and of Christ, and will reign with him one thousand years" (Rev 20:4-6).
