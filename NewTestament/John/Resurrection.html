IX. The Resurrection (20:1-31)

The empty tomb is a sign that Jesus has triumphed over sin and death. In the tomb lies not a lifeless body because Jesus has risen and taken on a new glorious body. Jesus has shattered the chains that held humanity captive, offering a path to salvation. His death is not just a personal victory, but a universal victory. It is the beginning of God's plan to renew and transform all of creation, healing its wounds and ushering in the kingdom of God. The resurrection declares that Jesus is the eternal and living Christ, and the Lord of all creation.

A. The Empty Tomb (20:1-10)

On Sunday morning while it was still dark, Mary Magdalene went to the tomb and discovered that it was empty (1). Supposing that they had moved the body, she ran to tell Peter and John that she didn't know where they had taken him (2). After hearing the news, Peter and John ran to the tomb, which they likewise found empty, and upon walking in discovered the linen cloths and face cloth from Jesus' body lying neatly there (3-8). The disciples return home believing that Jesus had risen from the dead but not yet understanding how this fulfills the Old Testament (9-10).

B. Appearance to Mary Magdalene (20:11-18)

Mary Magdalene, who was weeping, moved to see inside the tomb, and saw two angels in white, one where the head of the body of Jesus had been and one where his feet had been (11-12). The angels asked her why she was weeping, and she told them it was because they had taken away her Lord (13). At that moment, she turned around and saw Jesus, who she mistook to be a gardener because of his resurrected body, who asked why she was weeping and whom she was seeking, and again she asked about the location of the body of Jesus (14-15). Right after that, Mary recognizes Jesus and is sent to tell the disciples that he will ascend to the Father soon, so she goes and annouces to the disciples that she has seen the Lord (17-18).

C. Appearance to the Disciples (20:19-23)

A few hours later, Jesus entered the place where the disciples were despite the locked doors (19). He showed them his hands and his side where he had been pierced and commisions them to go out and proclaim the gospel (20-21). Jesus breathes on them and tells them to recieve the Holy Spirit (22). The gospel they are to proclaim is about the forgiveness of sins (23).

D. Appearance to Thomas (20:24-31)

Thomas refuses to believe the resurrection until Jesus appears again to the disciples and allows Thomas to touch the wounds (24-27). This leads to Thomas' great confession of his faith in Jesus: "My Lord and my God!" (28). John tells us that his purpose in writing is that we also may have faith in Jesus, believing that Jesus is the Christ, the Son of God, and that by believing we might have life in his name (29-31).
